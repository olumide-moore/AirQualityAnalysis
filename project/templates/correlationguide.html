Correlation Guide

The Pearson correlation is used to quantify the linear relationship between the two pair s of variables that are observed together.

The correlation coefficient ranges from -1 to 1.

A value of 1 indicates a perfect positive relationship, while a value of -1 indicates a perfect negative relationship.

A value of 0 indicates no relationship between the variables.

Average Interval: is the time interval for averaging the data before calculating correlation. Shorter intervals may reveal fine-scale variations, while longer intervals can smooth out short-term fluctuations.
